## Quality Standards for New Riddles

### Must Have:
1. **Single Correct Answer** - No ambiguity, one clear solution
2. **Age-Appropriate** - 6-12 year olds should understand
3. **Fair Clues** - Enough information to solve logically
4. **Subject-Relevant** - Connects to math/science/language arts/social studies
5. **Testable** - Child's answer can be validated programmatically

### Should Avoid:
- ‚ùå Multiple valid answers
- ‚ùå Requires specific cultural knowledge
- ‚ùå Too many steps (max 2-3 logic steps)
- ‚ùå Wordplay that depends on exact phrasing
- ‚ùå Answers that are "any number" or "anything"

### Good Riddle Patterns:

**Math:**
- Number properties with unique solution (even/odd, digit puzzles)
- Shape characteristics
- Classic math wordplay (zero, infinity concepts)

**Science:**
- Observable phenomena (shadows, weather, light)
- Physical properties (holes, water, fire)
- Natural processes

**Language Arts:**
- Letter/word manipulation with single answer
- Linguistic paradoxes
- Reading/writing tools

**Social Studies:**
- Geography features (maps, rivers, roads)
- Civic symbols (flags, coins)
- Historical/cultural icons
